Vegetarian Diet

A vegetarian diet is chosen for many different reasons. The typical vegetarian diet has no meat at all and does not allow foods that were made as the by-product of animal slaughter. There are many variations however that allowand ban different foods.

Foods Avoided:

Meat

Gelatin

Foods made with rennet

Some Foods Allowed:

Fruits

Vegetables

Tree nuts

Grains

Legumes

Gluten-Free Diet

Gluten-free diets are generally used by people with the autoimmune disorder Celiac Disease. People with Celiac Disorder cannot have foods with the protein gluten in them. Gluten is found in wheat-related grains.

Foods Avoided:

Wheat

Rye

Barley

 

Some Foods Allowed:

Grains without gluten include:

Rice

Buckwheat

Corn

Sorghum

Low-Sodium Diet

Low-Sodium diets are used by people with cardiovascular diseases or those who want to try to prevent it. Low-sodium diets lower blood pressure for both poeple with hypertension and normal blood pressure. The diet requires between 1,500 and 2,400 mg of sodium each day (2,300 mg is about 1 tsp).

Foods Avoided:

Foods with high amounts of sodium such as:

Pork products

Soy sauce

Most fast food

Potato chips

Canned vegetables

Some Foods Allowed:

Fresh and frozen vegetables

Fruit

Fresh or frozen beef, lamb, and fish

Eggs

Yogurt

Peanut Allergy

Peanut allergies cause the body's immune system to overreact. Peanut allergies are considered one of the most severe food allergies and is the most common cause for fatal food-related anaphylaxis.

Foods Avoided:

Peanuts

Foodswith peanut oil in them

Mandelonas

Peanut flour

Some Foods Allowed:

Anything that does not contain peanuts or peanut by-products such as peanut oil and peanut flour.
